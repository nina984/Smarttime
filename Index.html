Alex's Journey: From Confusion to Excellence

Meet Alex, a student at the National Higher School of Mathematics. Despite attending classes regularly, his grades were disappointing. Confused and stressed, he couldn't figure out what was wrong.

Late Night Realization

One night, Alex stayed up late reflecting and searching. He discovered the real reason behind his failure: poor time management. He was wasting time on the wrong tasks and had no clear study structure.

The Eisenhower Matrix

Alex found a powerful solution: The Eisenhower Matrix, named after President Dwight Eisenhower. It divides tasks into four categories:

  • Important & Urgent: Do it now.
  • Important but Not Urgent: Plan it.
  • Not Important but Urgent: Delegate it.
  • Not Important & Not Urgent: Eliminate it.

Using this method, Alex reorganized his time wisely and focused on what truly mattered.

Academic Transformation

After adopting time management strategies, Alex's academic performance improved remarkably. He was more confident, focused, and consistent.

Powerful Time Management Tips for Students

  • Use a digital planner like Google Calendar to plan your week in advance.
  • Follow the Pomodoro technique: 25 minutes of focus + 5 minutes of rest.
  • Prioritize your tasks using the Eisenhower Matrix.
  • Reduce distractions by turning off notifications during study time.
  • Track your progress weekly and reflect on what worked.

Final Advice: How to Excel at ENSM

  • Never skip a lecture. Notes from your teacher are golden.
  • After class, rewrite your notes in your own words.
  • Make a weekly schedule with fixed slots for each subject.
  • Group similar subjects (e.g., Math + Logic) in the same session.
  • Study in an environment free from interruptions (library or study room).
  • Do not just solve exercises—understand how they are built and how to generalize them.
  • Ask questions in class or on forums if you’re stuck.
  • Teach your classmates: if you can explain it, you understand it.

Remember, consistent effort beats perfection. Start small, stay disciplined, and watch your academic life transform just like Alex’s!
